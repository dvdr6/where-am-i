⚠️ What This Means For You

01

No Permission Needed

Your IP address, general location, browser, operating system, screen size, and language are all collected automatically the moment you load any webpage — no popups, no consent buttons, nothing. Every website on the internet does this.

02

Targeted Attacks

Malicious actors use your IP data to craft geographically targeted phishing emails, fake bank pages, and scam calls that sound legitimate because they mention your city, your ISP, or your region. Familiarity breeds trust — and exploits it.

03

Browser Fingerprinting

Combining your browser version, OS, screen resolution, timezone, and language creates a near-unique "fingerprint" that can track you across the web — even in private/incognito mode — without any cookies.

04

ISP-Level Surveillance

Your Internet Service Provider (ISP) can see every domain you visit. In many countries, ISPs are legally required to log and retain this data and hand it over to authorities — or sell anonymized versions to advertisers.

🛡️ How To Protect Yourself

🔐

Use a Trusted VPN

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) masks your real IP address by routing your traffic through an encrypted server in another location. Choose audited, no-log providers like Mullvad or ProtonVPN.

🧅

Use Tor Browser

The Tor Browser routes your traffic through multiple encrypted relays across the world, making it extremely difficult to trace your real IP or location. Ideal for maximum anonymity.

🦊

Use a Privacy Browser

Firefox with uBlock Origin, Brave Browser, or LibreWolf significantly reduce fingerprinting, tracker scripts, and data leaks compared to mainstream browsers.

🔎

Use Private Search Engines

Switch to DuckDuckGo, Startpage, or SearXNG. Google and Bing log every search query and tie it to your account, IP, and device fingerprint.

📵

Avoid Sketchy Links

A single visit to a malicious page is enough to capture your IP and device data. Always check URLs carefully, avoid shortened links, and never open email attachments from unknown senders.

🧩

Use DNS-over-HTTPS

Enable encrypted DNS in your browser or OS settings (Cloudflare 1.1.1.1 or NextDNS) to prevent your ISP from snooping on the domains you visit via unencrypted DNS queries.
